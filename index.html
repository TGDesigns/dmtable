Prologue

The Sword Coast, a land of vast beauty and diverse cultures, stretches along the western shores of FaerÃ»n in the Forgotten Realms. Its cities and towns teem with life, bustling with adventurers seeking glory, riches, and the thrill of daring quests. Yet, beyond the surface, shadows stir, and the winds carry whispers of impending change.

Amidst this coastline, the factions of the realm maneuver in the shadows, each with their own agenda. Many factions including, The Lords' Alliance, a coalition of influential cities and leaders, seeks to uphold order and rally the forces of good and justice. The Harpers, a clandestine organization with eyes and ears in every corner, strive to uncover secrets and protect the innocent. The Zhentarim, enigmatic and inscrutable, manipulate events for their own gain but in what they believe is good for the realms. And many others working deep in the shadows or for the good of fae'run.

In the celestial realm, the gods watch over their domains. Temples and shrines dedicated to these deities stand as beacons of hope, offering solace to those seeking divine intervention. From the stalwart Helm, guardian of the innocent, to Tymora, lady luck herself, the gods' influence shapes the destinies of mortals.

The Sword Coast, however, is not merely a realm of factions and divine influence. Mighty dragons, majestic and fearsome, soar through the skies, their presence a testament to their power. Ogres, Bugbears and goblins, lurk in murky corners. Dwarves, Elves, and elementals all make their presence known. Some creatures are benevolent, bestowing wisdom or aid upon those they deem worthy. Others, driven by greed and aggression, leave a trail of destruction in their wake. Legends speak of ancient artifacts, imbued with extraordinary power, hidden within treacherous dungeons or guarded by formidable foes.

In present day on the sword coast, Rumors have begun to circulate, like ripples in a pond, speaking of mysterious rumblings in the depths of the wilds. Hushed voices speak of colossal beings, their presence felt in the tremors of the earth and the distant echoes of thunderous footsteps. But the nature and purpose of these massive figures remain shrouded in uncertainty.

As these whispers spread, fear tinges the air, and the people of the Sword Coast find themselves on the edge of anticipation. The ground quivers with each passing day, leaving villages and settlements on alert, uncertain of what the future may hold.

And so, as destiny weaves its tapestry, your paths converge. United by a common purpose, you find yourselves drawn into a journey that will lead you across the breadth of the Sword Coast. From the bustling streets of Waterdeep, the City of Splendors, to the ancient ruins of Neverwinter, and even to the untamed wilderness of the Savage Frontier, your adventure will take you to both familiar and unknown territories.

With each step you take, you will encounter allies and adversaries, face trials and tribulations, and uncover the secrets that lie within the realm. The Sword Coast awaits, a canvas for your heroic exploits and a crucible for your character's growth. The fate of the realms rests in your hands, and the choices you make will shape the course of this world.

Prepare yourselves, valiant adventurers, for an extraordinary odyssey through the Sword Coast. The stage is set, the curtain rises, and your epic quest begins.

Previously on DND...

In our latest session, our adventurers found themselves on the High Road, a path leading from Nightstone to Waterdeep. Yelton, a clever and curious gnome wizard, crossed paths with Celeste, a formidable and enigmatic aasimar barbarian. Intrigued by a plume of smoke in the distance, they decided to investigate its source.

Meanwhile, in the renowned Grey's Glory Tavern in Waterdeep, named after the famous Harshnag's Adventuring Company Force Grey, Durk, a half-orc wizard, and Sister Althea, a wise and compassionate old human monk, overheard a conversation between Liam Fallbright, the Dragonborn bartender, and Markham Southwell, the Sheriff of Triboar. Markham sought assistance in exploring the troubled village of Nightstone, which housed his sister Semile. A group was to convene at the tavern's central fountain the next morning to embark on this perilous journey.

As night fell, Durk and Althea departed the tavern, with Althea extending an act of kindness by providing a nourishing meal to a homeless woman. The following morning, they met Markham at the fountain, where negotiations ensued. Durk requested payment, Althea sought an escort for their travels, and Markham agreed to a fee of 8 gold after the completion of the task.

The group converged at the entrance to Nightstone, joined by Celeste and Yelton, and under Markham's lead, ventured into the abandoned village. The devastating aftermath of giant attacks became evident, with boulders strewn about, wreaking havoc upon the once-peaceful settlement. A quiet eeiry vibe filled the air of this destroyed village only broken by the sound of a incessant ringing bell coming from the assumed village temple. However, their attention was quickly drawn to the sight of two worgs feasting on a lifeless dog.

With newfound determination, our heroes engaged in their first combat encounter, valiantly fighting the worgs and attracting the attention of goblins, who emerged from the ransacked houses. The skirmish was intense, with weapons clashing and spells crackling through the air.

The worgs bit and clawed their way into those that struck them. Markham was mostly able to fend off their attacks while Celeste lost the upperhand (Quite litterally) after falling into a recent excavation site in the middle of the town square. Fighting at a lower vantage point with success, by cutting off the toes of her foe, put her in harm's way as a worg threw her like a ragdoll across the square, knocking her unconcious.

Yelton, Durk, and Althea tried their hand at besting the wave of goblin attackers. Yelton held back a goblin with a ray of frost, slowing its approach into the fray. Durk and Althea charged two others and quickly eviscerated the two foes as if they were worthless meat sacks.

In the end, two worgs and two goblins fell, while one goblin was captured for further interrogation.

Undeterred by the battle's toll, minus a few scratches and a little health potion from Markham to alleviate some of Celeste's ailments, the group continued to explore Nightstone, seeking any sign of evil and hoping to locate Semile, Markham's sister. Sadly, their search led them to Semile's house, where they discovered her lifeless body crushed under a massive boulder. Althea, with the inert goblin in toe, remained by Markham's side as he took a minute to himself, while the rest of the group proceeded to investigate the local inn.

Peeking through a dust covered window in the back of the inn, Durk found the kitchen in disarray with another vile creature wreaking havoc in the room. He sent a vocal projection in the room that confused the goblin, he didnt know who or what it was but only that it frightened him and forced him to flee into the awaiting ambush of the adventurers. Trying to make a daring escape, Durk tossed the Gnome Yelton like a sack of flour in an effort to stop his pursuit. Although, sustaining some cuts and bruises on the tumble, Yelton annihilated the poor fleeing monstrosity.

Inside the inn, Celeste's keen senses guided her to a dining room, where she discovered a large rock jutting through the ceiling and an ominous crossbow bolt sticking from a goblin's chest pinned to a broken table. As she pondered its origin with curiosity piqued, Celeste called out, and a woman above revealed herself.

Returning to the Late Southwell's Residence, Durk presented Althea with belongings recovered from the kitchen goblin's ransacking finding the unconcious goblin returning to the waking world. While Althea attempted to calm the captive goblin, Durk's presence compelled the creature to speak. The goblin, sent by his boss Hark, admitted to pillaging the village on the orders of their leader. He revealed that the villagers sought refuge in nearby caves after a supposed giant attack, giving the goblins free reign to pillage the abandoned settlement.

Back upstairs, Celeste and Yelton discovered a barricaded door obstructed by debris. Working together, they cleared the path and encountered Kella Darkhope, accompanied by her loyal flying snake pet, trapped in the bedroom. Yelton's revelation of his own familiar, a whimsical unirabbit struck a chord of familiarity between them.

With Kella's rescue and the goblin's revelations, our adventurers reached a momentary pause in their quest. The mysteries of Nightstone deepened, and their determination to uncover the truth grew.

Ember Tweetings

Appearance: Ember is a striking young woman with a vibrant presence. Her most notable feature is her lustrous blue hair, reminiscent of her famous father, Flint Tweetings. Despite her age, her half-elf lineage gives her a timeless beauty that captivates those who meet her. Ember's youthful visage may make her look younger than her actual age, but her commanding presence and strength reveal a maturity beyond her years.

Attire: Ember proudly wears her father's old blue denim vest, a treasured heirloom that she has meticulously tailored to fit her form. The vest carries the weight of her family's legacy and symbolizes her commitment to carrying on her father's spirit. Beneath the vest, she dons practical yet stylish clothing befitting a leader. Her attire reflects her confidence and practicality, allowing her to move freely while still commanding respect.

Personality: Ember exudes strength, confidence, and determination. Her upbringing as the daughter of a renowned NPC has shaped her into a natural leader. She carries herself with authority, earning the respect and loyalty of those around her. Ember is known for her unwavering dedication to protecting Triboar, and she takes her responsibilities as Lord Protector seriously. Despite her strong demeanor, she has a compassionate heart and strives to bring justice and prosperity to her people.

Abilities: Ember possesses physical and mental prowess, blending the grace and agility of her elven heritage with the resilience and endurance of her human lineage. Her training as a leader and warrior has honed her combat skills, making her a formidable opponent on the battlefield. Additionally, she possesses a sharp intellect and strategic mind, enabling her to make wise decisions in times of crisis.

Background: As the daughter of the legendary NPC Flint Tweetings, Ember grew up in the shadow of her father's reputation. From an early age, she was exposed to tales of his heroic deeds, and she aspired to follow in his footsteps. As she came of age, Ember embraced her destiny and dedicated herself to protecting the town of Triboar. Through rigorous training and her inherent abilities, she earned the trust of the town's inhabitants and was eventually appointed as their Lord Protector.

Motivation: Ember's primary motivation is to honor her father's legacy and protect the people of Triboar. She is driven by a deep sense of justice and the desire to ensure the town's prosperity and safety. Her devotion to her role as Lord Protector is unwavering, and she constantly seeks ways to improve the town's defenses, maintain order, and foster growth.

With her blue hair, tailored denim vest, and an air of strength and confidence, Ember Tweetings commandsattention wherever she goes. Her dedication, leadership, and beauty make her an inspiring figure and a formidable force to be reckoned with as the Lord Protector of Triboar.

The ground beneath them trembles, but to their surprise, the source of the commotion is not what they expected. Gears and pistons protrude from its sides, whirring and clanking in a synchronized symphony of motion. Second Floor

You find yourselves in a massive, opulent but purposeful chamber. Its quirkey as it is messy and mechanical as much as it is lavish. Adorned on each of its four full walls are grand murals and intricate carvings. These artistic masterpieces depict a couple things: The wall closest to the orb - a full map of what you recognize to be Fae-run yet written in giant tongue ( 6 Runes marking the location of giant settlements). The 3 other murals showcase Giant Prowess. One portrays a formidable cloud giant, dressed in ornate armor, standing atop a floating cumulus cloud. The giant's eyes gleam with lightning, and a swirling storm rages behind them, casting dramatic lightning bolts across the sky.. Another, showcases a breathtaking aerial view of a floating archipelago of islands, each with lush green landscapes and cascading waterfalls. Bridges and pathways connect the islands, creating a harmonious and serene scene. The clouds surrounding the islands, a familiar image of castles floating atop each. And Finally.. A self portrait of Zephyros himself..Except like Really buff and much ....much younger. Flanking each of the murals on its walls are two extremely large sconces but with a magical orb of light rather than a flame illuminating the room, however still flickering as if to give of the warms of an actual flame. The two additional walls opposite each other stands too very ornate balconies with large entrically decorated wooden lattice screen doors preventing most of the wind from the brisk air shooting through the chamber accompanied by some very interestingly decorated curtains as if the deisgn was crudely hand painted to look sophisticated yet not quite achieved.

In the center of this vast chamber sits a crystalline orb. Zephyros walks by it placing his palm upon the orb and swirling his finger as if scrolling before tapping on what seems to be a random spot on the orb. You feel a soft pull and realize the castle has stopped from hovering and is now lifted high in the sky and begins its travel north. Zephyros explains it is this magnificent orb that maintains the delicate unity of the castle's elemental forces, allowing it to soar among the clouds to any destination of its choosing while interacting with the counterweights within the walls, as he carresses his own mural - "keeping it upright".

Inside this massive chamber you also notice a couple other interesting features. A colossal lab bench with beakers and makeshift microscopes looking very carefully at what now seem extremely underwhelming small folk items like a lantern and a satchel. A large chest opened brimming with a ranodm collection of items like normal sized decorative curtains that resemble a similar pattern yet,

There's a massive book case with several large books overwhelming in size but also a stack of normal sized books that barely measure in stature. A couple oversized cushions and a rug and what looks to be a glass tower, in actuality a large smoking pipe. in the center of this lounging area.

On the cieling a massive chandelier hangs delicately and another hole into the room above. Zepheros asks politely on first glance of this hole - "I do ask that you politely allow me some privacy in my chambers while you visit my home" as he points to the hole and then sways his arms to the cushions "but you are more than welcome to take repose amongst the library"
